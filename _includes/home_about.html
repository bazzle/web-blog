About me

I'm a creative minded designer and developer who is crazy about the user interface, and how it’s built.

I've worked within a variety of sectors ranging from fashion to commercial property to government and charity.

I take a proactive approach when taking on a project, and ask myself and others the relevant questions. I think about who the users are, who the client is and where their expectations lie. I think in a subjective user-centred way.

Did I already say I’m crazy about web interfaces? And I love designing them. I like to use core aspects of design such as colour, shapes, typography, white-space and animation as tools to create structure, meaning and sense of place.

I live and breathe CSS and javascript. I believe a well designed CSS structure and using SASS and a methodology like BEM is vital for a website or app. I stay up to date with development trends as much as I can.

I have a solid understanding of SEO and accessibility. To help the website reach as many people as possible – whether disabled, partially sighted, non-sighted, non-human.

So what inspires me? It’s often said that good design goes un-noticed, and bad design stands out like a sore thumb. Well like everyone else, I get frustrated at badly designed interfaces when I use them in day-to-day life. In fact I go out of my way to not use a website or app if the interface is badly designed, even if I do like the brand or product.

In our fast paced and on-demand world we don’t use interfaces to be impressed by good design, but to do a specific thing. An interface that does what I ask of it in a painless and comfortable way is what inspires me. I’m less inspired by the latest web design trends that are superficial and have no utility. Every element in a design should have a reason to exist. My mentality is often less is more. On the other hand, if more means adding an element to make an interaction more usable, then I’m all for it.
